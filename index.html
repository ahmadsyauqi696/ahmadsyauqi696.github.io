Gracilarria

Seaweed

Gracilaria is a red seaweed species that thrives in tropical marine waters and is commonly cultivated along the Indonesian coastline. Widely known for its high agar content, Gracilaria is a premium raw material for the food industry, especially for agar-agar, jellies, and thickeners. In addition to food, it’s also used in cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology

  • Moisture : <=35%
  • Impurity : <=5%

Sargassum

Seaweed

Sargassum is a brown seaweed rich in alginate, a compound that plays a key role in industrial applications such as fertilizers, animal feed, bioplastics, textiles, and pharmaceuticals

  • Moisture : <=20%
  • Impurity : <=8%

Cottonii

Seaweed

Eucheuma Cottonii is one of the most in-demand seaweed types in the world due to its rich carrageenan content, which is widely used as a gelling, thickening, and stabilizing agent. Our Cottonii is cultivated sustainably in the clean waters of Indonesia and handled with strict quality control to ensure natural color, low moisture, and high gel strength—ideal for food, cosmetics, and pharmaceutical applications

  • Moisture : <=35%
  • Impurity : <=5%

Ulva Lactuca

Seaweed

Ulva lactuca (sea lettuce) is a green seaweed rich in plant-based protein, minerals, and antioxidants. It is used as a raw material in health food, supplements, natural cosmetics, organic feed, and biofertilizer industries. Ideal for businesses seeking natural and sustainable solutions

  • Moisture : <=13%
  • Impurity : <=3%
